Raj Das

Sounak Raj Das (tenor) is currently a student at the University of Michigan studying voice performance and electrical engineering, and due to graduate in December of 2018.

On the electrical engineering side of things, Raj's strong interests include DSP, particularly in the realm of image processing, and bio/health informatics. Programming skills include experience with C, C++, SQL, and Python. Past experience in this field include employment by IngeniousMed, a company dedicated to simplifying hospital revenue cycles, as part of their Business Intelligence team. Here, he created an HTML parsing program and assisted in the creation of a semi-automated tool to follow data through the IngeniousMed databases and the ETL process to find out which filters were being applied to customer data before viewing. Additionally, he recently completed a senior design project with collaborators Alexander J. Martin and Matthew Kenney using image processing techniques to detect and classify the gestures of the Kodaly Hand Sign System on a Raspberry Pi without the aid of Machine Learning.

On the voice performance side of things, Raj currently serves as a staff singer at St. John's in Detroit, and has served as the tenor section leader at West Side United Methodist Church in Ann Arbor, Michigan. He has also performed with the University of Michigan for four years (2014-2018), and has had solos on works with them including "Haste Thee, Nymph" (by Handel), "Let Us Plant Our Gardens Now" (by Dominick DiOreo, primiered 2016), and "Out of the Cradle" (Iman Habibi, primiered 2018). He has performed such roles as Cyril in Princess Ida, and enjoys genres such as oratorio and lieder.
